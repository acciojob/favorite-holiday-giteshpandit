Kyoto, Japan


Kyoto, once the capital of Japan, is a city on the island of Honshu. It's famous for its numerous classical Buddhist temples, as well as gardens, imperial palaces, Shinto shrines, and traditional wooden houses. It's also known for its formal traditions such as kaiseki dining and geisha female entertainers.

  1. Fushimi Inari Taisha - The head shrine of the kami Inari, known for its thousands of vermilion torii gates.
  2. Kinkaku-ji (The Golden Pavilion) - A Zen Buddhist temple covered in gold leaf.
  3. Arashiyama Bamboo Grove - A picturesque bamboo forest that offers a tranquil walk.
  4. Kyoto Imperial Palace - The former ruling palace of the Emperor of Japan.
  5. Gion District - A famous geisha district, filled with shops, restaurants, and teahouses.
  • Kaiseki - A traditional multi-course Japanese dinner.
  • Yudofu - Tofu simmered in hot water, often served with dipping sauces.
  • Matcha - Powdered green tea, often used in traditional tea ceremonies.
  • Shojin Ryori - Vegetarian cuisine originally derived from the dietary restrictions of Buddhist monks.
  • Kyo-wagashi - Traditional Kyoto sweets, often enjoyed with tea.
